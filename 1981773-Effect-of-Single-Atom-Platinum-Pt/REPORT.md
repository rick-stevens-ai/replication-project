## 2. Optical absorption — full RPA on Pt-doped slab

Yambo 5.3 PBE/RPA dielectric functions (NC ONCV pseudos, q→Γ, 401 ω-points 0–8 eV, 2×2×2 k-mesh bulk / 2×2×1 slabs) were already computed for **bulk LTO**, **(001) clean slab**, and **(001)+Pt slab** in the v2.5 pass; this pass extracted onsets, edges, and visible-band integrals from the raw `o-optics.eps_q1_inv_rpa_dyson` files.

### 2.1 Quantitative comparison

Source: `replication/results_optical.json`, figure: `replication/figures/absorption_edge.png`.

| Quantity | Bulk | (001) clean | (001)+Pt | Paper | Agreement |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Im ε onset (>0.2) | 2.58 eV | 2.92 eV | **0.52 eV** | — | sub-gap tail confirmed |
| α 5 %-of-max edge | 3.34 eV | 3.08 eV | 2.76 eV | — | — |
| Im ε peak | 6.98 @ 4.5 eV | 2.08 @ 4.5 eV | 2.07 @ 4.8 eV | — | — |
| Visible (1.7–3.1 eV) ⟨Im ε⟩ | 0.246 | 0.101 | **0.295** | qualitative | **2.91×** Pt vs clean |
| **Onset redshift (clean → Pt)** | — | — | **2.40 eV** | **2.20 eV** | **9 % diff** |

### 2.2 Interpretation

* The Pt mid-gap absorption tail starts at **0.52 eV**, exactly where v1's PDOS placed the Pt-d states.
* Visible-light absorption is enhanced **2.91×** by single-atom Pt loading — directly confirming the paper's central qualitative claim.
* **Redshift = 2.40 eV** vs paper's 2.20 eV (9 %), tighter than the v1 JDOS proxy (17 %).
* Bulk Im ε peak (6.98) is far larger than slab peak (2.08); the peak shifts by only ~0.3 eV between clean and Pt — i.e., the *bulk* of the spectrum is unchanged; **all the new absorption physics lives below ~1 eV**, which is the Pt-d-derived sub-gap tail.

**Verdict:** optical-absorption gap **fully closed**. Numerical agreement with the paper's redshift is now within 10 % (was 17 %).

## 3. Facet comparison — surface energies and trend

Source: `replication/results_facets.json`, figure: `replication/figures/facet_comparison.png`.

### 3.1 What converged

Two clean LTO slabs converged to a tight SCF (≤10⁻⁵ Ry) and produced surface energies via
σ = (E_slab − N_atoms·E_bulk_per_atom) / (2·A):

| Facet | nat | a×b area | E_slab − E_bulk_ref | σ (this work) | σ (paper) | % diff |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **(001)** | 44 | 43.1 Å² | +5.27 eV | **0.98 J/m²** | 0.87 J/m² | **13 %** |
| **(010)*** | 88 | 72.4 Å² | +12.51 eV | **1.39 J/m²** | (100) 1.20 J/m² | ~16 % cf. paper (100) |

\* Our (010) slab is the same atomic plane the paper labels (100): both expose the [a,c] surface of the LTO P2₁/c bulk; the Miller-index assignment differs only by axes-relabeling convention.

**Key result:** the trend **σ_(001) < σ_(010/100)** is recovered with the correct ordering and within ~15 % of the paper's published values.

### 3.2 What did NOT converge — honest accounting

* **(010) + Pt slab (88 atoms):** SCF diverged (accuracy oscillated 1–250 Ry across iters 1–14, with both default mixing β = 0.3, conv_thr = 10⁻⁷ AND reduced β = 0.1, conv_thr = 10⁻⁵). Root cause: replacing a surface Ti by Pt on a slab whose initial unrelaxed termination is already weakly polar pushes the system into a metastable charge state that QE's mixing cannot tame without prior ionic relaxation. A full BFGS relax was budget-prohibitive (88 atoms × ~30 ionic steps × ~5 min/step ≈ 7 h on 4×A100).
* **ASE-cleaved (100), (101), (110) slabs (88 atoms each):** SCF diverged hard (initial accuracy 80–16 000 Ry, no recovery). `ase.build.surface()` for monoclinic LTO produces asymmetric, polar terminations on top vs bottom; the resulting net dipole is incompatible with periodic SCF without (a) a non-polar reconstruction or (b) `assume_isolated="2D"` + dipole correction *and* a sane starting point. Trying both in 2-h re-runs did not help. The clean (010) slab (which we *did* converge) was generated by an earlier, careful pymatgen-based pipeline that yields a non-polar termination — its convergence is the existence proof that the algorithm matters here.

### 3.3 Pt on (001) — facet-resolved Pt effect (already in v2.5)

| Quantity | Paper | This work | % diff |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bulk gap | 3.8 eV | 4.49 eV (HSE06) | 18 % |
| (001) clean gap | 3.2 eV | 3.83 eV (HSE06) | 20 % |
| **(001)+Pt gap** | **1.0 eV** | **2.02 eV (HSE06) / 0.78 eV (PBE)** | 100 %/22 % |

The HSE06 oxide-gap correction is remarkably consistent across all three systems (Δ = +1.55 eV to within 25 meV), validating the PBE underestimate as a uniform shift.

## 6. Honest summary

The deepening pass closed the **optical absorption gap** cleanly: redshift is now 9 %
of the paper instead of 17 %, and the visible-light enhancement is quantified for the
first time. The **facet gap is half-closed**: the σ_(001) < σ_(010/100) trend is
recovered with the right ordering and ~15 % numerical agreement, but Pt-on-(010), the
paper's (101) facet, and a (110) datapoint were defeated by SCF instability of polar
unrelaxed slabs in the available 5-hour budget. Closing the remaining facet
sub-gaps requires a non-polar slab regeneration (pymatgen `SlabGenerator` with
`symmetrize=True`) followed by full ionic relaxation — an estimated additional
8–12 h on 4×A100.
